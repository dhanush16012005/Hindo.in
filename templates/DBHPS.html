DBHPS Exams Overview

The Dakshina Bharat Hindi Prachar Sabha (DBHPS) conducts a series of exams to promote and assess Hindi language proficiency. Here is an overview of the eight key exams offered and how Hindo instructors will teach students at each level:

1. Prathamic (Primary Level)

Level: Lower Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Basic Hindi vocabulary and grammar.
  • Simple sentence construction.
  • Reading and comprehension of basic Hindi passages.
  • Basic Hindi writing skills.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Interactive Lessons: Use of interactive activities to build a strong foundation in basic Hindi.
  • Visual Aids: Incorporating flashcards, pictures, and videos to make learning engaging.
  • Practice Tests: Regular tests to monitor progress and provide feedback.
  • Conversational Practice: Emphasis on speaking and listening through simple conversations.

2. Madhyama (Middle Level)

Level: Lower Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Intermediate Hindi grammar and vocabulary.
  • Composition and essay writing.
  • Reading comprehension of moderately complex passages.
  • Introduction to Hindi literature and poetry.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Grammar Exercises: Focused grammar exercises to build intermediate skills.
  • Writing Workshops: Structured workshops to improve essay and composition writing.
  • Literary Discussions: Discussions on simple literary texts to enhance comprehension.
  • Quizzes and Assessments: Regular quizzes to assess understanding and progress.

3. Rashtrabhasha (National Language Level)

Level: Lower Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Advanced Hindi grammar and vocabulary.
  • Detailed study of Hindi prose and poetry.
  • Advanced comprehension and critical analysis.
  • In-depth essay writing and translation exercises.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Advanced Literary Analysis: Detailed analysis of prose and poetry.
  • Critical Thinking Exercises: Activities to develop critical thinking and analytical skills.
  • Translation Practice: Regular practice with translation exercises.
  • Comprehensive Feedback: Detailed feedback on writing assignments to improve quality.

4. Praveshika (Entrance Level)

Level: Higher Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Comprehensive Hindi grammar and vocabulary.
  • Study of classical and modern Hindi literature.
  • High-level comprehension and critical analysis.
  • Advanced composition and creative writing.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • In-depth Literary Study: Intensive study of selected literary works.
  • Creative Writing Sessions: Workshops focused on enhancing creative writing skills.
  • Analytical Discussions: Group discussions to analyze and interpret texts.
  • Personalized Coaching: One-on-one sessions to address individual needs.

5. Visharadh Poorvardh (Advanced Part 1)

Level: Higher Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Advanced Hindi linguistics and grammar.
  • Study of selected Hindi literary works.
  • In-depth analysis of prose and poetry.
  • Advanced essay writing and critical analysis.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Focused Study Groups: Group study sessions for collaborative learning.
  • Advanced Grammar Workshops: Workshops to master complex grammatical concepts.
  • Literary Critique: Sessions dedicated to the critique of literary works.
  • Regular Assessments: Frequent assessments to track progress and areas of improvement.

6. Visharadh Uttaradh (Advanced Part 2)

Level: Higher Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Continued study of advanced Hindi literature and linguistics.
  • Detailed analysis and interpretation of texts.
  • High-level essay and composition writing.
  • Preparation for professional use of Hindi.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Intensive Study Sessions: Deep dives into complex literary texts.
  • Advanced Writing Practice: Exercises to hone high-level writing skills.
  • Professional Application: Training for the practical application of Hindi in professional settings.
  • Mentorship: Guidance and mentorship for academic and professional growth.

7. Praveen Poorvardh (Proficiency Part 1)

Level: Higher Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Mastery of Hindi grammar and vocabulary.
  • Extensive study of Hindi literature.
  • Advanced comprehension and analysis.
  • In-depth essay writing and translation.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Master Classes: Expert-led classes on advanced topics.
  • Literary Workshops: Workshops focused on intensive literary study.
  • Translation Exercises: Regular practice with translation of complex texts.
  • Feedback and Improvement: Continuous feedback to refine skills.

8. Praveen Uttarardh (Proficiency Part 2)

Level: Higher Exam

Basic Syllabus Outline:
  • Expert-level proficiency in Hindi.
  • Specialized study of linguistics and literature.
  • Research and thesis writing.
  • Advanced translation and interpretation.
How Hindo Instructors Teach:
  • Individualized Study Plans: Custom study plans tailored to each student's needs.
  • Research Guidance: Mentorship for conducting research and writing theses.
  • Advanced Interpretation Practice: Practice sessions for translation and interpretation.
  • Professional Development: Training for the professional application of Hindi skills.
